## [Chip-based quantum network over 540 km fiber demonstrated](https://www.ithome.com/0/966/525.htm) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Pan Jianwei's team demonstrated a chip-based quantum key distribution network using twin-field QKD (TF-QKD) over 540 km of fiber, achieving a secure key rate of 2.93 bps, published in Nature Photonics on June 19, 2026. This breakthrough significantly advances practical quantum communication by integrating key components onto photonic chips, reducing system complexity and enabling scalable, secure networks for metropolitan and intercity distances. The transmitter chip combines a Si3N4 micro-ring resonator self-injection locked laser (100 Hz linewidth) and a thin-film lithium niobate (TFLN) photonic integrated chip with 25 GHz modulation bandwidth, 2.6 V half-wave voltage, and 34 dB extinction ratio. The network uses a quantum leaf-spine architecture to support flexible user connections.

rss · IT之家 · Jun 20, 07:51

**Background**: Quantum key distribution (QKD) enables theoretically unconditional secure communication by using quantum mechanics to share encryption keys. Twin-field QKD (TF-QKD) overcomes the rate-distance limit of conventional QKD, but requires extremely stable single-photon interference between two independent lasers, making chip integration challenging.

## [3D fiber micro-tweezer achieves 100,000x force of optical tweezers](https://36kr.com/newsflashes/3861209206740228?f=rss) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers from Anhui University and the University of Science and Technology of China developed a 3D fiber micro-tweezer on a commercial optical fiber using femtosecond laser fabrication, achieving over 100,000 times the output force of conventional optical tweezers. The work was published in Nature on June 17, 2025. This breakthrough enables high-precision, low-damage manipulation of micrometer-scale objects, including opaque particles and single cells, with forces far exceeding traditional optical tweezers. It opens new possibilities for micro-assembly, biomedical sampling, and minimally invasive procedures in confined spaces. The device measures only 38×38×61 μm³ and integrates a rigid photoresist micro-jaw with a temperature-responsive hydrogel 'muscle' doped with silver nanoparticles. It is fabricated in one step via two-photon polymerization 3D printing, and its force-to-mass ratio reaches ~340 μN/mg, one to two orders of magnitude higher than previous fiber-integrated micro-grippers.

rss · 36氪 · Jun 20, 08:42

**Background**: Optical tweezers use a tightly focused laser beam to trap and manipulate microscopic particles, but they typically generate forces on the order of piconewtons and cannot handle opaque objects. Traditional mechanical micro-grippers can provide larger forces but are bulky and complex. This new fiber micro-tweezer overcomes these limitations by combining light transmission, photothermal conversion, and soft material actuation on a single fiber tip.

## [UK Scientists Overcome Laser Noise in Quantum Sensors](https://www.ithome.com/0/966/546.htm) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A UK team led by Imperial College London demonstrated a differential atom interferometer that cancels laser phase noise, achieving quantum-limited sensitivity even under artificially added noise. The results were published in Nature on June 17, 2026. This breakthrough removes a major obstacle to building kilometer-scale atom interferometers, which could detect gravitational waves from the early universe and dark matter signals beyond the reach of current detectors. It paves the way for the AION project and similar international efforts. The team used two clouds of ultracold strontium-87 atoms as separate interferometers, sharing the same ultra-stable clock laser. By comparing their measurements, common laser noise was canceled, recovering signals even when single-device data was overwhelmed. The system operated at the standard quantum limit.

rss · IT之家 · Jun 20, 11:43

**Background**: Atom interferometers use the wave nature of atoms to make ultra-precise measurements, but they rely on laser pulses that introduce phase noise far larger than the target signals. The differential measurement technique cancels this common noise, enabling detection of faint signals from dark matter or gravitational waves. The AION project aims to build long-baseline atom interferometers in the UK.

## [CAS Breaks 3D DRAM Record: 4-Layer 2T0C with 400s Retention](https://www.ithome.com/0/966/543.htm) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Researchers at the Institute of Microelectronics, Chinese Academy of Sciences, demonstrated the first 4-layer 3D 2T0C DRAM using IGZO, achieving a record data retention of 400 seconds and 3 bits per cell. The work was accepted at VLSI 2026. This breakthrough enables high-density, high-bandwidth memory integration directly on logic chips, addressing the memory wall for AI and HPC applications. It offers a path to replace traditional SRAM and off-chip DRAM with a more scalable solution. The 3D DRAM uses a dual-gate IGZO 2T0C cell with a vertical word-line architecture, optimized for read margin and stability. The 4-layer stack was fabricated using a single-step high-layer 3D integration scheme, achieving 3 bits per cell and 400-second retention.

rss · IT之家 · Jun 20, 10:33

**Background**: Traditional DRAM uses a 1T1C (one transistor, one capacitor) cell, which faces scaling limits. 2T0C DRAM eliminates the capacitor, using two transistors for storage and readout, and can be built with IGZO, a transparent oxide semiconductor that allows low-temperature processing and integration on top of logic chips. 3D stacking further increases density by layering memory cells vertically.

- [How LLMs Choose Words: Logits, Temperature, Top-P](https://machinelearningmastery.com/the-statistics-of-token-selection-logits-temperature-and-top-p-walkthrough/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

  > This article provides a practical walkthrough of the statistical mechanisms behind token selection in large language models, specifically explaining logits, temperature scaling, and top-p (nucleus) sampling with concrete examples. Understanding these parameters is crucial for practitioners who want to control the creativity, coherence, and randomness of LLM outputs, directly impacting applications like chatbots, content generation, and code assistants. The article covers how logits are raw scores from the model's final layer, temperature scales logits before softmax to adjust randomness, and top-p sampling dynamically selects a subset of tokens whose cumulative probability exceeds a threshold p.

- [Build Semantic Search with Transformers.js and Sentence Embeddings](https://machinelearningmastery.com/building-semantic-search-with-transformers-js-and-sentence-embeddings/) ⭐️ 7.0/10

  > A tutorial demonstrates how to implement semantic search in the browser using Transformers.js and sentence embeddings, moving beyond keyword matching to understand user intent. This approach enables more relevant search results for users, especially for queries with synonyms or typos, and runs entirely client-side for privacy and low latency. The tutorial uses the all-MiniLM-L6-v2 model from Hugging Face, which generates 384-dimensional embeddings, and computes cosine similarity between query and document embeddings.

- [Continuous Batching Boosts LLM Inference Efficiency](https://machinelearningmastery.com/serving-multiple-users-at-once-how-continuous-batching-keeps-llm-inference-efficient/) ⭐️ 7.0/10

  > The article explains how continuous batching dynamically schedules requests and uses ragged batching to improve LLM inference efficiency, contrasting it with static batching and providing code examples. Continuous batching can achieve 2-3x throughput gains over static batching, making LLM serving more cost-effective and responsive for real-world applications with variable request patterns. The article covers static batching's inefficiency due to padding and idle GPU cycles, then introduces continuous batching with iteration-level scheduling and ragged batching to eliminate padding, with a full implementation example.

- [Building a Context Pruning Pipeline for Long-Running LLM Agents](https://machinelearningmastery.com/building-a-context-pruning-pipeline-for-long-running-agents/) ⭐️ 7.0/10

  > A new tutorial on MachineLearningMastery.com details how to build a context pruning pipeline for long-running LLM agents, using embedding similarity and top-K selection to manage conversation history and prevent context overflow. As LLM agents become more autonomous and long-running, context window overflow is a critical bottleneck that causes coherence loss and task failure. This practical technique enables agents to maintain performance over extended interactions without exceeding token limits. The pipeline embeds past conversation turns, computes cosine similarity with the current prompt, sorts by similarity, and selects the top-K most relevant turns to include in the pruned context. It also includes a base case for short histories and assembles the final context from the current prompt, most recent turn, and selected past turns.

- [Implementing Hybrid Semantic-Lexical Search in RAG](https://machinelearningmastery.com/implementing-hybrid-semantic-lexical-search-in-rag/) ⭐️ 7.0/10

  > A tutorial demonstrates how to implement hybrid search in RAG systems by combining BM25 lexical search with semantic search using Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF). Hybrid search improves retrieval quality by balancing exact keyword matching and semantic understanding, reducing hallucinations in RAG-based applications. The tutorial uses BM25 for lexical search and dense embeddings for semantic search, fused via RRF to produce a single ranked list. It provides practical code examples for production-ready RAG systems.
