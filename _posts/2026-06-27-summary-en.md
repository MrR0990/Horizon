## [New Tantalum Alloy Doubles Strength at 2000°C, Published in Nature](https://www.ithome.com/0/969/466.htm) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Researchers at Xi'an Jiaotong University developed a boron-mediated oxide-dispersion-strengthened (B-ODS) tantalum alloy that achieves a tensile yield strength of 200 MPa at 2000°C, doubling that of conventional tantalum alloys. The work was published in Nature on June 27, 2026. This breakthrough addresses the critical need for structural materials that can withstand ultra-high temperatures above 2000°C, which is essential for next-generation aerospace components like rocket nozzles and hypersonic vehicle parts. The alloy's combination of room-temperature ductility and ultra-high-temperature strength could enable new design possibilities for extreme environments. The B-ODS tantalum alloy exhibits a tensile yield strength of 200 MPa at 2000°C and 100 MPa at 2400°C, with a room-temperature tensile strength over 800 MPa and elongation of 35%. It also shows superior creep resistance compared to traditional refractory alloys, and its temperature capability under 100 MPa load is about 500°C higher than existing tantalum alloys.

rss · IT之家 · Jun 27, 10:22

**Background**: Tantalum has a high melting point (~3000°C) but conventional tantalum alloys suffer from insufficient strength at ultra-high temperatures. Oxide dispersion strengthening (ODS) involves dispersing fine oxide particles in a metal matrix to enhance high-temperature strength. The team used a boron-mediated in-situ oxidation reaction to control the size and distribution of oxide particles, achieving a uniform dispersion that significantly improves performance.
